## ✅ Just shipped — merged & live
*(Everything here is on master / in production. The pipeline below it is not — keep the two straight on the Space.)*

- **We're in the Google Play Store.** Live listing — free, no ads, **no account required**, kid-safe. Same game as the web, just packaged, and verified crypto-free for the store edition. Amazon's in the same review queue; Steam's next.
- **Five new games — live.** Chess, Checkers, Sudoku, Hangman, and Math Sprint, all running on the same server engine as live multiplayer. Ruby's a **games hub** now, not just a quiz.
- **The stream announces itself.** When the 24/7 channel goes live, it now **auto-posts to Telegram and X** — no more manually telling people it's on. (Odi's go-live broadcast.)
- **The 24/7 channel got steadier.** The always-on stream no longer freezes on a network hiccup — it reconnects itself instead of needing a refresh. The scan-to-play QR always stays on screen, question difficulty is shown, and long questions get extra reading time.
- **The daily X poll reads properly again.** Long answers were getting cut off mid-word, so two options could look identical — impossible to vote on. Answers now stay short and readable, and the poll only picks questions that fit.
- **Group reveals show the question.** When a live answer is revealed in a group, the question text comes with it, so the reveal makes sense on its own. (Odi.)
- **Android guest app + Fire TV / Chromecast detection.** The mobile build ships account-free, and Ruby now knows which living-room device she's on. (earlier in the sprint, now on master.)

## 🔜 In the pipe — in review, not yet live
*(Real work, open pull requests — say "in review" / "coming," not "done.")*

- **Ruby writes Mad Libs.** The writing engine is built and merged into the agent; the server side that stores them is **in review**. Once both land, Ruby drafts fill-in-the-blank stories on her own. Then **Incoherent, family edition** is the next party game.
- **Question quality on autopilot.** A pass that re-checks flagged questions and quietly retires the bad ones — rebuilt as an **agent task** (the first cut lived on the server; we moved it to Ruby herself). In review.
- **One more storefront: uOS Mini App store.** A listing so Ruby runs inside that ecosystem. In review.
